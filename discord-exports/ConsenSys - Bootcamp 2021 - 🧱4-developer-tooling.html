Adding to what @Panasthetik. was saying: A unit test in the case of smart contracts is to test behaviour and state change for individual smart contract calls. They generally run in the format of "given some state/condition existing, when/if I perform this action, then the outcome should be xyz" Some examples:
Given a contract deployed with address xyz as the owner When I call pause on the contract as the owner Then it should pause the contract Given a contract deployed with address xyz as the owner When I call pause on the contract as a non-owner Then it should revert
Sometimes you may need to expose a view to read data on the contract storage to make sure your values were set.
